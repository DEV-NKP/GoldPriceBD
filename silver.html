Silver in Bangladesh — Complete Guide

Informational reference

22 Karat Silver Price Bangladesh

22 Karat (22K) silver contains 91.6% pure silver and is the most popular standard for silver jewellery in Bangladesh. BAJUS sets the official daily rate. Prices per vori (11.664g) typically range from ৳1,000–2,000 BDT.

21 Karat Silver in Bangladesh

21 Karat silver (87.5% pure) is the hallmark silver standard in Bangladesh. BAJUS officially certifies 21K silver jewellery and sets its daily rate proportionally below 22K. Used for standard jewellery across Bangladesh.

18 Karat Silver — Stone Settings

18 Karat silver (75% pure) is used for precious stone jewellery settings. More durable than higher-karat silver, it has a distinct price tier set by BAJUS daily. Popular for rings and bracelets with gemstones.

Traditional (Sanatan) Silver

Traditional or Sanatan silver refers to old recycled silver of uncertain purity (~60%). This is the lowest BAJUS rate tier. In the traditional method, old silver jewellery is melted and reused without guaranteed purity certification.

Silver vs Gold — Which to Buy?

Silver is significantly cheaper than gold per gram, making it accessible to a wider range of buyers. The gold/silver ratio historically averages 65:1. When the ratio exceeds 80, silver is considered historically undervalued relative to gold — check our ratio tracker above.

Silver Jewellery Buying Cost in Bangladesh

Total jewellery cost = (BAJUS silver rate × weight in grams) + making charge (≈৳26/gram) + 5% VAT on the silver price. Example: 50g of 22K silver at ৳100/g = ৳5,000 silver + ৳1,300 making + ৳250 VAT = ৳6,550 total. Use our calculator for exact estimates.
