Why Gold Price Increases in Bangladesh

6 Key Reasons | বাংলাদেশে সোনার দাম কেন বাড়ে?
Gold price increases in Bangladesh due to 6 main reasons: (1) International XAU spot price rises, (2) USD/BDT exchange rate depreciation, (3) Central bank gold buying globally, (4) Geopolitical tensions driving safe-haven demand, (5) Import duties and VAT increases, (6) Local demand surge during wedding season and Eid.

1. International Gold Spot Price (XAU/USD)

The biggest factor affecting gold price in Bangladesh is the international gold spot price. When global gold rises due to central bank buying, inflation fears, or geopolitical risk, BAJUS increases Bangladesh gold rates proportionally.

2. USD/BDT Exchange Rate Depreciation

Gold is priced in USD globally. When the Bangladeshi Taka weakens against the US Dollar, the same ounce of gold costs more in BDT. This is why gold price BD often rises even when international gold is stable.

3. Central Bank Gold Buying

Central banks worldwide (especially China, India, Russia) buying record amounts of gold reduces available supply and pushes prices higher. This institutional demand creates sustained upward pressure on gold prices in Bangladesh.

4. Geopolitical Tensions & Safe-Haven Demand

Wars, trade conflicts, and political instability drive investors toward gold as a safe-haven asset. Global uncertainties contribute directly to gold price increases in Bangladesh.

5. Import Duties & Government Taxes

Bangladesh imposes import duties and VAT on gold imports. When the government increases these taxes, the landed cost of gold rises, and BAJUS passes this cost to consumers. The 5% VAT on gold jewellery is mandatory nationwide.

6. Local Demand — Wedding Season & Festivals

Gold demand in Bangladesh peaks during wedding season (November-February), Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha, and Durga Puja. Prices often dip after festivals when demand falls. Understanding this seasonal pattern helps find the best time to buy gold in Bangladesh.

বাংলাদেশে সোনার দাম কেন বাড়ে?

বাংলাদেশে সোনার দাম বাড়ার প্রধান কারণ: আন্তর্জাতিক গোল্ড স্পট প্রাইস বৃদ্ধি, ডলারের বিপরীতে টাকার অবমূল্যায়ন, কেন্দ্রীয় ব্যাংকের সোনা ক্রয়, ভূ-রাজনৈতিক উত্তেজনা, আমদানি শুল্ক ও ভ্যাট, এবং বিয়ের মৌসুম ও উৎসবে চাহিদা বৃদ্ধি।

Why Gold is Better for BD Investors

Gold in Bangladesh offers: universal acceptance (every jeweller buys gold), high liquidity (can sell anywhere in BD), cultural value (essential for weddings), lower volatility, and proven wealth preservation over decades.

Why Silver Might Outperform Gold in BD

Silver offers: much lower entry cost per vori, higher industrial demand (electronics, solar panels, medical), higher percentage volatility (bigger gains possible), and lower making charges per gram. When the gold-silver ratio is above 50:1, silver is historically undervalued.

Gold-Silver Ratio — What It Means for BD

The gold-silver ratio tells you how many ounces of silver equal one ounce of gold. Historically: ratio below 40 = silver expensive, ratio above 60 = silver cheap. Check the live gold-silver ratio in the International Market section.

Recommended Strategy for BD Investors

For Bangladesh investors: allocate 70% to gold (wealth preservation, cultural needs, liquidity) and 30% to silver (growth potential, low entry cost). Buy gold during mid-week dips after festivals. Always verify prices with BAJUS official rates on GoldPriceBD.

Best Time to Buy Gold in Bangladesh

Monthly & Seasonal Guide | বাংলাদেশে সোনা কখন কেনা ভালো?
The best time to buy gold in Bangladesh is: (1) Mid-week (Tuesday-Wednesday) when BAJUS rates are typically lower, (2) After major festivals when demand drops, (3) When international gold spot dips from recent highs, (4) When USD/BDT rate stabilizes or taka strengthens, (5) March-April and August-September are historically low-demand months. Avoid buying before Eid and wedding season (Nov-Feb).

Weekly Pattern — Best Day to Buy Gold in BD

Based on 90-day BAJUS price data: Tuesday and Wednesday typically have the lowest gold prices in Bangladesh. Thursday-Friday prices rise as weekend demand builds. Saturday-Sunday can spike due to wedding purchases. Buy mid-week for the best gold price BD.

Monthly Guide — When Gold is Cheapest in BD

Cheapest months: March-April (post-wedding season, before Eid shopping), August-September (monsoon, low demand). Most expensive months: November-February (peak wedding season), Ramadan/Eid period, October (Durga Puja). Plan major purchases during low-demand months.

Festival Impact on Gold Price in BD

BAJUS gold rates typically rise before Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha, and during wedding season. Prices dip in the weeks after major festivals when demand falls. Smart buyers purchase gold after festivals, not before. Use GoldPriceBD's Market Insights to track the best buying day.

International Price Timing for BD Buyers

For BD buyers: watch international gold price and USD/BDT rate on GoldPriceBD. Buy when both international price AND USD/BDT are simultaneously down — this creates a double discount on gold price in Bangladesh.

বাংলাদেশে সোনা কখন কেনা সবচেয়ে ভালো?

বাংলাদেশে সোনা কেনার সেরা সময়: সপ্তাহের মাঝামাঝি (মঙ্গলবার-বুধবার), বড় উৎসবের পরে যখন চাহিদা কমে, আন্তর্জাতিক সোনার দাম কমলে, এবং ডলারের বিপরীতে টাকা শক্তিশালী হলে। বিয়ের মৌসুম এবং ঈদের আগে সোনার দাম সবচেয়ে বেশি থাকে।

About Gold & Silver Prices in Bangladesh

Complete Guide | Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)

What is BAJUS?

BAJUS (Bangladesh Jewellers Samity — বাংলাদেশ জুয়েলার্স সমিতি) is the official trade association of gold and silver jewellers in Bangladesh. They publish the official daily gold and silver rates that all registered jewellers must follow. Their office is at Bashundhara City Shopping Complex, Dhaka.

What is 1 Vori (ভরি)?

1 Vori (also called Tola or Bhori) equals 11.664 grams and is the primary unit for gold and silver measurement in Bangladesh. 1 Vori = 16 Ana = 96 Roti = 960 Point. It is equivalent to 1 Tola used in India and Pakistan.

Gold Karats in Bangladesh

22K (91.67% pure) — Most popular for jewellery in Bangladesh. 21K (87.5%) — Hallmark standard. 18K (75%) — Used for diamond/stone settings. Traditional/Sanatan — Old jewellery, approximately 60% pure, no fixed standard.

How to Calculate Jewellery Cost

Total cost = (BAJUS gold rate × weight in grams) + making charge (≈৳300/gram for gold, ৳26/gram for silver) + 5% VAT on the gold value. Use our calculator for an instant accurate estimate.
