Gold in Bangladesh — Complete Guide

Informational reference

22 Karat Gold Price Bangladesh

22 Karat (22K) gold contains 91.6% pure gold and is the most popular standard for gold jewellery in Bangladesh. BAJUS sets the official daily rate. The price per vori (11.664g) is typically in the range of ৳200,000–260,000 BDT depending on international gold markets.

21 Karat Gold in Bangladesh

21 Karat gold (87.5% pure) is the hallmark standard used in Bangladesh. It is slightly less pure than 22K and therefore priced lower. BAJUS officially certifies 21K gold jewellery and sets its daily rate proportionally below 22K.

18 Karat Gold — Diamond Settings

18 Karat gold (75% pure) is commonly used for diamond and precious stone jewellery settings in Bangladesh. It is more durable than higher-karat gold and has a distinct price tier set by BAJUS daily.

Traditional (Sanatan) Gold

Traditional or Sanatan gold refers to old recycled gold of uncertain purity (approximately 60%). This is the lowest BAJUS rate tier. In the traditional method, old gold jewellery is melted to make new ornaments with no guaranteed purity standard.

Gold Measurement Units in Bangladesh

Bangladesh uses the Vori (ভরি) = 11.664 grams as the primary gold unit. 1 Vori = 1 Tola = 16 Ana = 96 Roti. Gold is also traded per gram internationally. Our calculator supports all units including troy ounce (31.1035g).

Gold Jewellery Buying Cost in Bangladesh

Total jewellery cost = (BAJUS gold price × weight in grams) + making charge (≈৳300/gram) + 5% VAT on the gold price. Example: 10g of 22K gold at ৳20,000/g = ৳200,000 gold + ৳3,000 making + ৳10,000 VAT = ৳213,000 total.
